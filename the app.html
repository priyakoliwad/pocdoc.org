About Pocket Doctor

progressing healthcare one step at a time

The Problem

The World Health Organization estimates that more than half (52%) of all deaths in low-income countries in 2015 were caused by the so-called “Group I” conditions, which include communicable diseases, maternal causes, conditions arising during pregnancy and childbirth, and nutritional deficiencies. In 2015, 303, 000 women in the developing world died during and following pregnancy and childbirth, most of which occurred in low-resource settings, and most could have been prevented. Additionally, virtually all (99%) newborn deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries. A major contributing factor to these unnecessary dethats is the fact that people can’t identify serious sympotoms, and do not revice help in a timely manner.

THE solution

How do we help mohters identify symptomds in their baby before its too late? And how can a family with liitle to no education protect themself from preventable health issues? The answer Pocket Doctor: a web app that helps people idenitffy serious symptoms before it is too late. This android web app is accessible via phone or computer and is usable for any language with infographics and audio files. This allows people form all over th eworld to have access to our information. This app identifies serious symtoms, teaches people how to clean wounds, and hoe to clean and store water.
Will this app be any use? In the developoing world, more people have phones than toliets. Additonally, the Pew Research Ceanter said that “...as of 2015, 54% of the developing world had access to internet or had a smartphone” This number is rising and we aim to reach every person around the world.
